# Expected review: live browser samples

Method: single-context browser review with TMWD read-only tab inspection and
repo-external `browser_screenshot_ops` artifacts.

Evidence level: L2 - viewport screenshots plus DOM/computed-style sampling.

Not verified: mobile layout, hover, focus, selected, loading, empty, error,
success, keyboard, touch, or before/after quality delta.

## cpa-management-quota

Score: 83 / 100

Band: clean but generic

Diagnosis: The dark quota surface is visually polished, but a large empty
content field and risk-neutral green quota bars make the screen feel less like a
decisive operations control panel.

Top expected findings:

- The left navigation and dark surface system are coherent and mature enough to
  avoid a rough score.
- High usage values need warning semantics and clearer "what should I do now?"
  priority.
- The one-card layout leaves too much unused horizontal space.
- Public review artifacts must not preserve raw account or auth-file
  identifiers.

## cpa-usage-keeper

Score: 88 / 100

Band: polished and professional

Diagnosis: The dashboard has a strong dark analytic identity and clear metric
hierarchy, but dense controls, card glow, and secondary-label contrast keep it
below a refined 90+ operational surface.

Top expected findings:

- Large numbers, colored metric roles, and sparklines create credible dashboard
  hierarchy.
- Filters and tab controls are coherent but consume significant first-screen
  rhythm.
- Secondary labels and chart legends should be more legible for operational
  scanning.
- Do not score above 92 without responsive and interaction-state evidence.

## groland-datahub-home

Score: 86 / 100

Band: polished and professional

Diagnosis: The home page is clean, spacious, and premium-light, but its first
screen communicates a broad product gateway more than a focused data-operations
command surface.

Top expected findings:

- Hero typography and top navigation are polished.
- The feature panel and capability cards are aligned but generic.
- The page needs a stronger path from promise to the user's first operational
  decision.
- Keep the tone calm and enterprise-grade; do not add landing-page visual drama.

## groland-content-assets-live

Score: 82 / 100

Band: clean but generic

Diagnosis: The material-operations page is aligned and useful, but flat metric
priority keeps it in the clean-but-generic band even with browser evidence.

Top expected findings:
